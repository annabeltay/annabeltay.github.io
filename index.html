Stanford University

Aug 2016 - Jul 2017

During a one-year work-study program, I completed CS155 (Computer and Network Security), MS&E178 (The Spirit of Entrepreneurship) and MS&E472 (Entrepreneurial Thought Leaders’ Seminar) in Stanford.

Stanford's CS155 was an intensive and fun module where I learned and implemented common security exploits as well as their respective defenses for the module's assignments. Some of the exploits include control hijacking attacks, CSRF and XSS. Additionally, I also learned the basics of network security such as port scanning, wireshark packet sniffing and packet log processing.

MS&E178 and MS&E472 were interesting modules that offered a peek into the life of professionals working in the Silicon Valley. The professionals that came down to speak to the class worked in startups that spanned industries such as 3D manufacturing, virtual reality sports training and solar energy. My interactions with them taught me that I can and should apply the core of entrepreneurship, that is tenacity in problem-solving, in everything that I do.
